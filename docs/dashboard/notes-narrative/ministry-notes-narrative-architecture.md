# HOPE Dashboard — Ministry Notes and Narrative Architecture

## Purpose

Define the Ministry Notes and Narrative architecture for the clean HOPE Dashboard.

The notes and narrative layer should help pastors and ministry teams preserve meaningful ministry context, pastoral observations, care continuity, follow-up history, and person-centered story notes across the ministry operating system.

The system should answer:

> What should we remember about this person, what ministry context matters, who recorded it, and how do we preserve narrative continuity with trust and care?

---

## Core Principle

Ministry notes are not database comments.

They are pastoral memory.

The system exists to preserve meaningful ministry context with dignity, clarity, confidentiality, and operational trust.

The backend owns canonical note identity, visibility semantics, audit history, sensitivity rules, and replay-safe narrative projections.

The frontend presents notes and narrative context with pastoral clarity.

---

## Experience Philosophy

The notes and narrative experience should reflect:

> A pastor’s heart with an engineer’s brain.

The experience should feel:

- thoughtful,
- trustworthy,
- confidential when needed,
- explainable,
- ministry-aware,
- and human-centered.

Notes should help ministry teams remember people well, not merely attach comments to records.

---

## Human Authority

Pastors and authorized ministry leaders remain the final authority regarding:

- note interpretation,
- pastoral context,
- sensitive narrative handling,
- visibility decisions,
- and ministry response.

The system may assist organization and recall but must never replace pastoral discernment.

---

## Canonical Backend Ownership

The backend must own:

- note identity
- author identity
- visibility semantics
- sensitivity level
- edit history
- audit trail
- canonical timeline linkage
- replay-safe narrative projections
- note relationship linkage
- permission boundaries
- retention semantics

The frontend must not invent note or narrative truth models.

---

## Note Domains

The notes and narrative architecture may support:

- pastoral notes
- follow-up notes
- care notes
- prayer context
- formation observations
- serving observations
- group leader notes
- communication notes
- household context notes
- operational ministry notes

---

## Narrative Context

Narrative context may include:

- pastoral observations
- relationship history
- ministry preferences
- encouragement opportunities
- care continuity
- follow-up outcomes
- formation context
- communication context
- serving or group context

Narrative context should be preserved with humility and care.

---

## Visibility and Sensitivity

Notes may require different visibility levels.

Examples:

- general ministry note
- team-visible note
- pastoral-only note
- confidential care note
- restricted administrative note

Visibility semantics must be canonical and backend-enforced.

The frontend must not independently decide note visibility.

---

## Timeline Integration

Notes and narrative activity should integrate directly with:

- Unified Timeline
- Visitor Detail
- FollowUp Intelligence
- Ministry Care and Prayer
- Ministry Communication
- Workflow Orchestration
- Identity and Household Architecture

Notes should appear as canonical ministry story events when visibility rules allow.

---

## AI Integration

Pastoral AI surfaces may assist by:

- summarizing permitted notes
- preparing pastoral briefings
- identifying continuity themes
- surfacing relevant historical context
- suggesting follow-up questions

AI must remain bounded by canonical backend note semantics and visibility rules.

AI should not summarize confidential notes unless explicitly permitted by governance.

---

## Explainability

Notes surfaces should answer:

- Who wrote this note?
- When was it written?
- What context does it belong to?
- Who can see it?
- Has it changed?
- What sensitivity level applies?
- What ministry story does it support?

Users should understand both narrative meaning and visibility boundaries.

---

## Pastoral Language

Notes language should remain:

- human,
- respectful,
- careful,
- clear,
- and ministry-centered.

Prefer:

- Pastoral note
- Follow-up note
- Care context
- Ministry observation
- Encouragement opportunity
- Confidential note

Avoid:

- Comment object
- Case annotation
- Behavioral record
- User issue log
- Narrative artifact
- Data attachment

---

## Sensitive Interpretation Boundaries

The system must not:

- expose confidential notes improperly
- summarize restricted notes without permission
- infer spiritual condition as fact
- create hidden narrative scoring
- automate sensitive pastoral conclusions
- replace human review

Notes systems must preserve trust, dignity, and confidentiality.

---

## Editing and Auditability

Note edits should preserve:

- author identity
- edit history
- timestamps
- visibility changes
- sensitivity changes
- related workflow or timeline context

Narrative trust depends on clear authorship and auditability.

---

## Empty States

If no notes exist, the dashboard should communicate:

> No ministry notes are currently available.

The experience should remain calm and non-assumptive.

---

## Guardrails

Notes and narrative architecture must remain:

- backend-governed
- explainable
- replay-safe
- auditable
- confidentiality-aware
- pastorally readable
- human-authority preserving
- thin-client friendly

The frontend must never become the canonical notes interpretation engine.

---

## Operational Trust

Ministry teams should trust that:

- notes are accurate,
- visibility boundaries are enforced,
- edit history is auditable,
- narrative context is preserved,
- and note projections reflect backend truth.

Trust matters more than note-taking sophistication.

---

## Experience Standard

Ministry Notes and Narrative Architecture should feel like a trusted pastoral memory system helping ministry teams remember people well, preserve care continuity, and coordinate ministry with wisdom.

The system should help ministry teams:

- remember important context,
- preserve pastoral continuity,
- coordinate follow-up,
- protect confidentiality,
- support care and formation,
- and care for people without reducing their story to data.
