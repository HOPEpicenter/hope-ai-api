# HOPE Dashboard — Pastoral AI Guidance Surfaces

## Purpose

Define the Pastoral AI Guidance architecture for the clean HOPE Dashboard.

The AI guidance layer should help pastors and ministry teams make better ministry decisions while preserving human authority, pastoral discernment, and canonical backend truth.

The system should answer:

> What ministry insight, recommendation, or guidance would help this person care for people more effectively right now?

---

## Core Principle

Pastoral AI is not autonomous ministry.

It is assisted pastoral guidance.

AI exists to support ministry leaders, not replace them.

The backend owns canonical ministry truth, workflow semantics, intelligence state, and orchestration state.

AI guidance must consume canonical backend contracts rather than invent alternate truth models.

---

## Experience Philosophy

The AI guidance experience should reflect:

> A pastor’s heart with an engineer’s brain.

The system should feel:

- calm,
- trustworthy,
- explainable,
- ministry-aware,
- non-manipulative,
- and pastorally supportive.

The experience should reduce uncertainty without pretending to replace discernment.

---

## Human Authority

Pastors and ministry leaders remain the final authority over:

- ministry decisions,
- care actions,
- spiritual discernment,
- workflow outcomes,
- escalation handling,
- and formation direction.

AI guidance must assist humans rather than override them.

The system must never imply spiritual authority.

---

## Canonical Backend Ownership

The backend must own:

- canonical visitor state
- timeline semantics
- workflow state
- orchestration state
- follow-up intelligence
- engagement interpretation
- formation state
- recommendation inputs
- replay-safe projections
- deterministic semantic models

AI surfaces consume canonical backend contracts only.

The frontend must not invent AI truth models.

---

## AI Guidance Domains

AI guidance may assist with:

- follow-up recommendations
- pastoral conversation preparation
- reconnect opportunities
- prayer follow-up reminders
- formation next-step suggestions
- workflow prioritization
- ministry summarization
- engagement insight surfacing
- care coordination suggestions
- pastoral briefing generation

---

## Allowed AI Behaviors

The system may:

- summarize canonical information
- explain workflow context
- recommend possible next actions
- identify care patterns
- surface relevant timeline context
- prioritize attention areas
- draft suggested communication
- identify unresolved workflows
- explain why recommendations exist

AI guidance should remain assistive and explainable.

---

## Disallowed AI Behaviors

The system must not:

- invent canonical truth
- override workflow state
- fabricate ministry history
- hallucinate pastoral events
- infer spiritual state as fact
- replace pastoral authority
- create hidden workflow state
- manipulate urgency semantics
- silently modify orchestration logic

AI guidance must remain bounded by canonical backend truth.

---

## Explainability

Every AI recommendation should answer:

- Why is this being suggested?
- What canonical signals contributed?
- What timeline context matters?
- What workflow context matters?
- How recent is the information?
- How confident is the system?

Users should never feel manipulated by opaque AI behavior.

---

## Timeline Integration

AI guidance should integrate directly with:

- Visitor Detail
- Unified Timeline
- FollowUp Intelligence
- Workflow Orchestration
- Formation Experience
- Care workflows

AI recommendations should always connect to canonical ministry story context.

---

## Workflow Integration

AI guidance may assist workflow coordination by:

- identifying stalled care
- recommending escalation review
- surfacing overdue follow-up
- identifying reconnect opportunities
- summarizing workflow history
- suggesting assignment adjustments

AI must not become the canonical workflow engine.

---

## Pastoral Language

AI-generated language should remain:

- human,
- respectful,
- calm,
- hopeful,
- and pastorally appropriate.

Prefer:

- May benefit from follow-up
- Might need encouragement
- Could be ready for next step
- Suggested reconnect opportunity
- Possible pastoral attention need

Avoid:

- High-risk disengagement subject
- Low-value participant
- Behavioral anomaly
- Engagement failure pattern
- Automated care enforcement

---

## Confidence and Trust

AI surfaces should communicate confidence carefully.

Recommendations should distinguish between:

- confirmed canonical facts,
- backend-calculated intelligence,
- and AI-generated suggestions.

The user must always understand what is authoritative versus assistive.

---

## Sensitive Context Handling

AI guidance should treat:

- prayer requests
- care notes
- pastoral counseling
- escalations
- family situations
- sensitive workflows

with elevated caution and ministry sensitivity.

Not all contexts should receive AI summarization equally.

---

## Empty States

If no AI guidance is currently available, the dashboard should communicate:

> No additional pastoral guidance is suggested right now.

The experience should reinforce calm operational trust.

---

## Guardrails

Pastoral AI Guidance must remain:

- backend-governed
- explainable
- replay-safe
- auditable
- role-aware
- pastorally readable
- thin-client friendly
- human-authority preserving

AI must never become the canonical ministry authority.

---

## Operational Trust

Ministry teams should trust that:

- AI guidance is grounded in canonical backend truth,
- workflow context is accurate,
- timeline context is reliable,
- orchestration state is current,
- and recommendations remain explainable.

Trust is more important than AI sophistication.

---

## Experience Standard

The AI guidance layer should feel like a trusted ministry assistant helping leaders notice what matters while preserving human discernment and pastoral responsibility.

The system should help ministry teams:

- prepare better,
- respond faster,
- coordinate care responsibly,
- maintain operational clarity,
- and care for people intentionally without losing the human heart of ministry.
