# HOPE Dashboard — Ministry Care and Prayer Architecture

## Purpose

Define the Ministry Care and Prayer architecture for the clean HOPE Dashboard.

The care and prayer layer should help pastors and ministry teams understand prayer needs, pastoral care contexts, sensitive ministry situations, care workflows, and compassionate follow-up across the ministry operating system.

The system should answer:

> Who needs care, what prayer or pastoral context matters, who should respond, and how do we coordinate care with compassion and confidentiality?

---

## Core Principle

Care and prayer are not support tickets.

They are sacred ministry contexts.

The system exists to help ministry teams care for people with compassion, discretion, continuity, and pastoral wisdom.

The backend owns canonical care semantics, prayer request identity, sensitivity boundaries, escalation state, and replay-safe care projections.

The frontend presents care and prayer context with pastoral clarity.

---

## Experience Philosophy

The care and prayer experience should reflect:

> A pastor’s heart with an engineer’s brain.

The experience should feel:

- compassionate,
- calm,
- confidential,
- trustworthy,
- explainable,
- and ministry-aware.

Care workflows should help people feel seen, not processed.

---

## Human Authority

Pastors and authorized ministry leaders remain the final authority regarding:

- pastoral care decisions,
- prayer response,
- escalation handling,
- confidential context,
- care assignment,
- and ministry sensitivity.

The system may assist coordination but must never replace pastoral discernment.

---

## Canonical Backend Ownership

The backend must own:

- prayer request identity
- care request identity
- sensitivity semantics
- escalation state
- care assignment state
- prayer follow-up state
- care workflow linkage
- replay-safe care projections
- canonical care timeline identity
- care audit history
- confidentiality boundaries

The frontend must not invent care or prayer truth models.

---

## Care Domains

The care and prayer architecture may support:

- prayer requests
- pastoral care needs
- hospital visits
- grief care
- family care
- crisis escalation
- spiritual encouragement
- follow-up after absence
- care team assignment
- sensitive ministry notes
- confidential pastoral context

---

## Prayer Request Semantics

Prayer requests should preserve:

- person identity
- request context
- sensitivity level
- visibility rules
- assigned responder
- follow-up state
- created timestamp
- source
- update history
- canonical timeline linkage

Prayer semantics must remain backend-governed and explainable.

---

## Care Sensitivity

Care contexts may require different visibility levels.

Examples:

- general prayer
- team-visible care
- pastoral-only care
- confidential care
- escalation-required care

Sensitivity rules must be canonical and backend-enforced.

The frontend must not independently decide care visibility.

---

## Escalation Model

Care escalations may exist for:

- urgent pastoral response
- crisis-sensitive context
- prolonged unresolved care
- repeated prayer concerns
- sensitive family situations
- hospital or grief care
- safety-sensitive contexts

Escalations should remain compassionate, explainable, and access-controlled.

---

## Timeline Integration

Care and prayer activity should integrate directly with:

- Unified Timeline
- Visitor Detail
- FollowUp Intelligence
- Workflow Orchestration
- Pastoral AI Guidance
- Ministry Communication
- Operational Insights

Care and prayer events should appear as canonical ministry story events when visibility rules allow.

---

## Communication Integration

Care and prayer workflows may trigger or support:

- pastoral outreach
- prayer follow-up
- encouragement messages
- care team coordination
- escalation communication
- ministry leader notifications

Communication must respect sensitivity and consent semantics.

---

## AI Integration

Pastoral AI surfaces may assist by:

- summarizing care context when permitted
- preparing pastoral briefings
- suggesting compassionate follow-up
- identifying unresolved care
- explaining care workflow status

AI must remain bounded by canonical backend care semantics and visibility rules.

AI should not summarize confidential care contexts unless explicitly permitted by governance.

---

## Explainability

Care and prayer surfaces should answer:

- What care or prayer need exists?
- Who can see it?
- Who owns the next response?
- What has already happened?
- What sensitivity level applies?
- What follow-up is needed?
- What timeline context is available?

Users should understand both the care need and the confidentiality boundary.

---

## Pastoral Language

Care and prayer language should remain:

- compassionate,
- respectful,
- hopeful,
- careful,
- and pastorally appropriate.

Prefer:

- Prayer request
- Pastoral care need
- Needs encouragement
- Follow-up with care
- Care team assigned
- Confidential pastoral context

Avoid:

- Support ticket
- Case object
- Risk profile
- Behavioral incident
- Escalation target
- User problem state

---

## Sensitive Interpretation Boundaries

The system must not:

- expose confidential care improperly
- infer spiritual condition as fact
- automate sensitive pastoral decisions
- summarize restricted care without permission
- assign care severity without backend authority
- replace pastoral discernment

Care systems must preserve trust, dignity, and confidentiality.

---

## Empty States

If no care or prayer history exists, the dashboard should communicate:

> No care or prayer history is currently available.

The experience should remain calm, respectful, and non-assumptive.

---

## Guardrails

Care and prayer architecture must remain:

- backend-governed
- explainable
- replay-safe
- auditable
- confidentiality-aware
- pastorally readable
- human-authority preserving
- thin-client friendly

The frontend must never become the canonical care interpretation engine.

---

## Operational Trust

Ministry teams should trust that:

- care history is accurate,
- prayer requests are preserved,
- sensitivity boundaries are enforced,
- assignments are current,
- escalation state is reliable,
- and care projections reflect backend truth.

Trust matters more than care workflow complexity.

---

## Experience Standard

Ministry Care and Prayer Architecture should feel like a trusted pastoral care coordination system helping ministry teams respond with compassion, discretion, and continuity.

The system should help ministry teams:

- notice care needs,
- pray intentionally,
- follow up compassionately,
- coordinate sensitive care,
- preserve confidentiality,
- and care for people without losing the human heart of ministry.
