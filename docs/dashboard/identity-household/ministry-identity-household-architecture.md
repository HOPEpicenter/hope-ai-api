# HOPE Dashboard — Ministry Identity and Household Architecture

## Purpose

Define the Ministry Identity and Household architecture for the clean HOPE Dashboard.

The identity layer should help ministry teams maintain accurate person identity continuity, household relationships, family context, and canonical ministry story linkage across the ministry operating system.

The system should answer:

> Who is this person, how are they connected relationally, what household context exists, and how do we preserve canonical identity continuity over time?

---

## Core Principle

Ministry identity is not CRM contact management.

It is canonical relational ministry continuity.

The system exists to preserve accurate ministry identity, household relationships, family context, and longitudinal ministry story continuity while supporting pastoral care and operational trust.

The backend owns canonical identity semantics, household relationships, merge logic, linkage rules, and replay-safe identity projections.

The frontend presents identity context with pastoral clarity.

---

## Experience Philosophy

The identity experience should reflect:

> A pastor’s heart with an engineer’s brain.

The experience should feel:

- trustworthy,
- relational,
- explainable,
- stable,
- ministry-aware,
- and human-centered.

Identity systems should help ministry teams care for people accurately without turning people into abstract records.

---

## Human Authority

Pastors and authorized ministry leaders remain the final authority regarding:

- identity correction
- household relationships
- merge decisions
- family linkage interpretation
- pastoral relationship context
- and sensitive identity adjustments

The system may assist identity continuity but must not autonomously alter canonical identity truth without governance.

---

## Canonical Backend Ownership

The backend must own:

- person identity semantics
- household identity semantics
- family linkage rules
- canonical merge logic
- identity continuity rules
- replay-safe identity projections
- canonical relationship identity
- identity audit history
- duplicate detection semantics
- lifecycle identity continuity
- relationship graph ownership

The frontend must not invent identity truth models.

---

## Household Semantics

- canonical visitor history
- communication history
- formation progression
- workflow history
- timeline continuity
- care context
- ministry participation
- follow-up history

## Merge and Split Governance

The system may support:

- duplicate resolution
- identity merge workflows
- household reassignment
- family relationship correction
- contact reconciliation
- historical identity preservation

Merge and split operations must remain:

- auditable,
- deterministic,
- replay-safe,
- and operationally explainable.

The frontend must never execute canonical merge logic independently.

Identity continuity should preserve the canonical ministry story across all systems.

## Explainability

Identity systems should always answer:

- Why are these records linked?
- Why was a merge suggested?
- What canonical identifiers contributed?
- What historical continuity exists?
- What household relationships are recognized?
- What confidence exists in the linkage?

Users should never feel confused by opaque identity behavior.

---

## Pastoral Language

Identity language should remain:

- human,
- relational,
- respectful,
- explainable,
- and pastorally appropriate.

Prefer:

- Household connection
- Family relationship
- Shared ministry context
- Possible duplicate record
- Relationship continuity

Avoid:

- Entity consolidation
- Identity collapse
- Relationship normalization failure
- Contact object conflict
- Record dominance logic

---

## Sensitive Identity Boundaries

The system must not:

- infer inappropriate family relationships
- expose confidential identity context improperly
- automate sensitive relationship assumptions
- silently alter canonical identity state
- replace authorized human review
- create hidden relationship scoring systems

Identity governance must preserve dignity, trust, and operational safety.

---

## AI Integration

Pastoral AI surfaces may assist by:

- suggesting possible duplicates
- explaining household context
- summarizing ministry continuity
- surfacing relationship context
- preparing pastoral briefings

AI guidance must remain bounded by canonical backend identity semantics.

---

## Empty States

If little identity linkage exists, the dashboard should communicate:

> Additional household or relationship context is not currently available.

The experience should remain calm and non-assumptive.

---

## Guardrails

Identity and household architecture must remain:

- backend-governed
- explainable
- replay-safe
- auditable
- deterministic
- pastorally readable
- human-authority preserving
- thin-client friendly

The frontend must never become the canonical identity resolution engine.

---

## Operational Trust

Ministry teams should trust that:

- identity continuity is preserved,
- household relationships are accurate,
- merges are auditable,
- relationship context is explainable,
- and canonical ministry story linkage remains stable.

Trust matters more than aggressive identity automation.

---

## Experience Standard

Ministry Identity and Household Architecture should feel like a trusted relational continuity system helping ministry teams care for people accurately across households, family relationships, ministry participation, and long-term ministry journeys.

The system should help ministry teams:

- preserve relational continuity,
- reduce identity confusion,
- coordinate ministry context responsibly,
- maintain accurate household understanding,
- and care for people without losing the human heart of ministry.
