# HOPE Dashboard — Ministry Groups and Community Architecture

## Purpose

Define the Ministry Groups and Community architecture for the clean HOPE Dashboard.

The groups and community layer should help ministry teams understand belonging, group participation, community connection, leader oversight, discipleship relationships, and care visibility across the ministry operating system.

The system should answer:

> Where is this person finding community, who is walking with them, what group context matters, and how do we support belonging and discipleship responsibly?

---

## Core Principle

Groups are not attendance containers.

They are ministry communities.

The system exists to help ministry leaders understand relational belonging, discipleship connection, group health, and care context while preserving pastoral sensitivity and human authority.

The backend owns canonical group semantics, participation state, leader relationships, group lifecycle state, and replay-safe community projections.

The frontend presents community context with pastoral clarity.

---

## Experience Philosophy

The groups and community experience should reflect:

> A pastor’s heart with an engineer’s brain.

The experience should feel:

- relational,
- encouraging,
- trustworthy,
- explainable,
- ministry-aware,
- and human-centered.

Community systems should help people belong and grow, not merely fill group rosters.

---

## Human Authority

Pastors, ministry leaders, and group leaders remain the final authority regarding:

- group placement,
- pastoral fit,
- leader oversight,
- sensitive care context,
- discipleship interpretation,
- and community direction.

The system may assist visibility and coordination but must not replace ministry discernment.

---

## Canonical Backend Ownership

The backend must own:

- group identity
- group lifecycle state
- participation semantics
- leader assignment semantics
- community relationship state
- attendance linkage
- formation linkage
- replay-safe group projections
- group timeline identity
- group audit history
- group care visibility rules

The frontend must not invent group or community truth models.

---

## Group Domains

The groups and community architecture may support:

- small groups
- discipleship groups
- formation cohorts
- care groups
- prayer groups
- serving teams
- volunteer teams
- youth or family groups
- leadership groups
- ministry communities

---

## Group Identity

Group identity should preserve:

- group name
- group type
- lifecycle state
- leaders
- participants
- meeting context
- ministry purpose
- care sensitivity when relevant
- participation history
- canonical timeline linkage

Group identity must remain backend-governed and explainable.

---

## Participation Semantics

Participation may represent:

- active participant
- prospective participant
- invited participant
- paused participant
- former participant
- leader
- co-leader
- care-connected participant

Participation semantics must remain canonical and backend-owned.

---

## Belonging and Formation

Group participation may contribute to:

- community connection
- formation visibility
- discipleship progression
- pastoral care context
- serving readiness
- leadership development
- reconnect opportunities

The frontend must not independently convert group participation into formation state.

---

## Timeline Integration

Group and community activity should integrate directly with:

- Unified Timeline
- Visitor Detail
- Formation Journey Visualization
- Event and Attendance Architecture
- Volunteer and Serving Architecture
- Workflow Orchestration
- Operational Insights

Group participation should appear as canonical ministry story context when appropriate.

---

## Workflow Integration

Groups and community architecture may support:

- group onboarding workflows
- leader follow-up workflows
- participation follow-up
- reconnect workflows
- care escalation workflows
- formation pathway workflows
- leadership development workflows

Workflow state must remain backend-governed and replay-safe.

---

## Communication Integration

Group workflows may trigger or support:

- group invitations
- leader follow-up
- participant encouragement
- group reminders
- care coordination
- reconnect communication
- leader updates

Communication should remain relational and ministry-centered.

---

## AI Integration

Pastoral AI surfaces may assist by:

- summarizing community context
- identifying belonging opportunities
- suggesting possible next steps
- preparing leader briefings
- surfacing group care concerns
- explaining participation history

AI guidance must remain bounded by canonical backend group semantics.

AI must not autonomously place people into groups.

---

## Explainability

Group surfaces should answer:

- What group is this person connected to?
- What role or participation state exists?
- Who leads the group?
- What history exists?
- What care or formation context matters?
- What next step is recommended?
- What confidence exists in the connection?

Users should understand community context without guessing.

---

## Pastoral Language

Group and community language should remain:

- relational,
- encouraging,
- respectful,
- clear,
- and ministry-centered.

Prefer:

- Connected to a group
- Looking for community
- Leader follow-up recommended
- Joined formation cohort
- Group care context
- Community connection opportunity

Avoid:

- Roster object
- Participant utilization
- Community compliance
- Group throughput
- Low-performing participant
- Retention segment

---

## Sensitive Interpretation Boundaries

The system must not:

- shame people for not joining groups
- rank spiritual worth by group participation
- expose sensitive group care context improperly
- automate group placement without approval
- create hidden belonging scores
- replace leader discernment

Community systems should support belonging, care, and discipleship.

---

## Empty States

If no group or community history exists, the dashboard should communicate:

> No group or community history is currently available.

The experience should remain calm, respectful, and invitational.

---

## Guardrails

Groups and community architecture must remain:

- backend-governed
- explainable
- replay-safe
- auditable
- pastorally readable
- human-authority preserving
- leader-aware
- thin-client friendly

The frontend must never become the canonical group interpretation engine.

---

## Operational Trust

Ministry teams should trust that:

- group history is accurate,
- participation state is current,
- leader relationships are canonical,
- care context is explainable,
- and community projections reflect backend truth.

Trust matters more than group management sophistication.

---

## Experience Standard

Ministry Groups and Community Architecture should feel like a trusted belonging and discipleship context system helping leaders understand where people are connecting, who is walking with them, and how community can support formation and care.

The system should help ministry teams:

- support belonging,
- coordinate group participation,
- encourage discipleship,
- care through community,
- equip leaders,
- and care for people without reducing them to roster slots.
