# HOPE Dashboard — FollowUp Intelligence Experience

## Purpose

Define the FollowUp Intelligence experience for the clean HOPE Dashboard.

The system should help pastors and follow-up teams understand:

- who needs care,
- why they need care,
- what should happen next,
- and how urgently action is needed.

The experience should reduce missed care opportunities while preserving pastoral judgment and human ministry leadership.

---

## Core Principle

FollowUp Intelligence is not task automation.

It is pastoral care guidance.

The backend owns canonical intelligence semantics.

The dashboard presents care recommendations with clarity, trust, and ministry-centered language.

---

## Experience Philosophy

The experience should reflect:

> A pastor’s heart with an engineer’s brain.

The system should feel:

- helpful,
- trustworthy,
- calm,
- actionable,
- and ministry-aware.

The dashboard should assist people in caring for people.

---

## Primary Questions

The FollowUp Intelligence experience must answer:

1. Who currently needs attention?
2. Why do they need attention?
3. How urgent is the need?
4. What care action is recommended?
5. What has already happened?
6. Who owns the next step?
7. Can we trust the recommendation?

---

## Canonical Backend Ownership

The backend must own:

- follow-up scoring
- engagement evaluation
- attention prioritization
- lifecycle interpretation
- recommendation generation
- care state calculation
- urgency semantics
- deterministic ranking
- replay-safe intelligence projection

The frontend must not infer canonical care intelligence.

---

## Intelligence Inputs

Intelligence may consider:

- worship attendance patterns
- follow-up completion history
- engagement changes
- formation participation
- prayer requests
- timeline events
- care outcomes
- inactivity duration
- new visitor state
- family context
- ministry assignments
- integration events

---

## Intelligence Outputs

The backend may produce:

- recommended next actions
- pastoral attention indicators
- follow-up urgency levels
- engagement concern indicators
- reconnect opportunities
- celebration opportunities
- care escalation suggestions
- ministry assignment suggestions
- confidence indicators

---

## Recommended Care Actions

Examples:

- Send welcome text
- Schedule follow-up call
- Assign pastoral outreach
- Invite into formation pathway
- Check on prayer request
- Reconnect after absence
- Celebrate milestone
- Escalate urgent care need

The recommendations should feel pastoral rather than operational.

---

## Urgency Model

Urgency should be:

- deterministic,
- explainable,
- replay-safe,
- backend-calculated,
- and pastorally understandable.

Suggested urgency levels:

- Informational
- Recommended
- Important
- Urgent

The frontend must not invent urgency semantics.

---

## Explainability

Every recommendation should answer:

- Why is this being suggested?
- What signals contributed?
- What recent events matter?
- What care history influenced this?
- How fresh is the recommendation?

The user should never feel manipulated by opaque scoring.

---

## Pastoral Language

Prefer:

- Needs encouragement
- Has not connected recently
- Ready for next step
- Needs follow-up
- Showing healthy engagement
- Possible care concern

Avoid:

- Low-value engagement
- Risk object triggered
- Score degradation
- User inactivity breach
- Behavioral anomaly detected

---

## Human Authority

Pastors and ministry leaders remain the final authority.

The system assists.

The system does not replace pastoral discernment.

Recommendations should support ministry judgment rather than override it.

---

## Timeline Integration

FollowUp Intelligence should integrate directly with:

- Unified Timeline
- Visitor Detail
- Formation Experience
- Care workflows
- Ministry orchestration surfaces

Recommendations should always connect to canonical ministry story context.

---

## Empty States

If no recommendations exist, the dashboard should communicate:

> No follow-up concerns need attention right now.

The experience should reinforce confidence rather than imply inactivity.

---

## Guardrails

The experience must remain:

- backend-governed
- deterministic
- replay-safe
- explainable
- auditable
- pastorally readable
- thin-client friendly

The frontend must never become the canonical intelligence engine.

---

## Experience Standard

The experience should feel like a trusted pastoral assistant helping ministry leaders notice people who need care.

The system should help teams:

- respond faster,
- miss fewer people,
- maintain healthy follow-up,
- and care for individuals intentionally.
